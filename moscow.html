WHOIS.moscow

Just another fake hacker terminal used for cheap scares

So, WHOIS.moscow is this weirdly specific site that tries to look like a hacker tool for looking up domain info. It’s got that whole terminal vibe to trick you into typing something, but it's really just a front for a script that tries to mess with your browser and log you out of stuff.

The Belarus Fake-out

The funniest thing about this site is that it uses a .moscow domain to look all "Russian Hacker" style, but if you actually check the real WHOIS data for the site, the creator is just some guy from Belarus. He didn't even try that hard to hide it.

What happens when you use it?

The site starts off with a bunch of Russian text that looks like technical server logs or something. If you actually try to type a command into the prompt, the site goes crazy:

  • Force Fullscreen: It traps your browser in fullscreen mode so you can't easily close the tab.
  • The edramatica Logout: For some reason, the script specifically tries to force a logout on edramatica.com. Why edramatica? Literally nobody knows. Maybe the creator had a beef with them.
  • The Image Spam: It starts flashing a bunch of horror faces like Regan MacNeil, some Creepy ugly guy, Mr. Nelson, and Dr. Tongue.

The Goofy Screamer

The sound is the "Creator's Scream." It’s not even that scary—it’s actually kind of goofy. It sounds like someone just screamed into a really bad laptop mic with the gain turned up way too high, so it’s just distorted and loud. Most people on the wiki just find it funny at this point.

Trivia

  • Modern browsers usually block the logout script because of security rules, so it doesn't even work most of the time.
  • The Dr. Tongue image is edited to be super high-contrast so it looks like it’s glowing.
  • It's more of a prank site than a "horror" site because of how silly the audio is.

Stay safe out there. Don't trust random terminals.
