LieDetect.xo.je

From Screamer Fanon Wiki, the free encyclopedia of jumpscares

LieDetect.xo.je is a weird little site made by the same guy who ran the old ScareMail.tk. It's still online and it's basically a "lie detector" prank that tries way too hard to be realistic. The page greets you with some goofy text: "MOST TRULIEST APP ON THE FRICKING WORLD OF THE WORLDS!".

How it works

The site uses a mix of JavaScript and Flash to mess with you. As soon as you open the page, your browser asks for microphone permissions. This is just a script the creator put in to make it feel like a high-tech app, even though the Flash game itself doesn't actually use the microphone data at all.

The layout actually has two SWF files embedded:

  • Game SWF: This is the main lie detector interface where you "record" your voice.
  • The "Trap" SWF: A hidden file that is supposed to trigger a script to stop you from leaving the page until the screamer fires. Hilariously, this part is usually broken, so only the main game actually works.

The "Screamer" (The Cowroll)

To use the app, you have to "speak" into your mic and then manually click a button to stop talking. Once you stop, a loading bar appears to "analyze" your voice.

While it's loading, it suddenly cuts to a cheap stock image of a COW. A loud "moo" sound effect plays and text appears on the screen saying:

"get cowroll'd!"

Editors note: It's not actually scary, just really loud and completely out of nowhere. It's more of a troll than a horror screamer.

Trivia

  • Since Flash is dead in most browsers, you need an emulator like Ruffle to see the cow at all.
  • The microphone request still scares some people because they think the site is actually recording them.
  • The .xo.je domain is a free subdomain, which fits the creator's "cheap prank" aesthetic perfectly.
