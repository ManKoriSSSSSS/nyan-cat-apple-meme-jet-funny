PlayScaryGames.fun

"Your #1 source for fan-made terrors and the maze series." — Site tagline.

PlayScaryGames.fun is a niche web portal dedicated to hosting "bait-and-switch" content, Flash screamers, and experimental horror apps. Unlike larger mainstream sites, this portal is known for hosting a high volume of fan-made rescripts and "reskin" games, making it a primary source for much of the content documented on this wiki.

Hosted Games Library

The Maze: Part 5 - A vermillion-themed reskin of the original Maze game featuring the Glimpse (SCP-087-B) screamer.
Noodles Recipe.gif - Disguised as a cooking tutorial, this file triggers a jump-scare midway through the recipe.
IQ Test - A standard "find the differences" or logic puzzle bait-and-switch.
Magic Eye - Requires the player to stare closely at a stereogram image before the screamer activates.
Steady Hand 2 - A sequel to a fanon series involving precise cursor movement.
The Maze: Part 6 [BROKEN] - Notable for being the only currently broken game on the site. The Flash file fails to load past the splash screen, meaning the actual jump-scare is currently inaccessible.
Spinning Cucumber - A simple animation that originally had its own wiki page; however, that page was deleted by moderators due to poor formatting and low-effort descriptions.
Spinning Donut - A similar low-effort animation often used as a testing ground for audio triggers.
How to write a scp article! - A meta-commentary game that baits writers with advice before flashing the "Glimpse" entity.
Red Pancake NSFW - Contains graphic content; restricted under wiki rule #1.
Fox transformation subliminal message - Uses pseudo-science bait to lure in specific sub-communities.
Online Audacity Analog! - A fake audio editing tool that triggers a screamer when the user clicks the "Play" button on a specific waveform.
Tank Simon Says - A military-themed memory game.
Scary Game - A generic title often used to host experimental or "test" screamers.

Trivia

  • The site is often cited as a "Wild West" for fanon content because of its lack of strict quality control.
  • Users frequently use The Maze: Part 6 as a "safe" game to link to others as a joke, since the broken code prevents the jump-scare from actually firing.
  • Spinning Cucumber is a controversial topic among wiki editors; while the game exists on the site, it currently lacks a compliant wiki entry due to previous formatting issues.
