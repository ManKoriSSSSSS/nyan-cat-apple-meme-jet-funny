Online Audacity Analog!

From Screamer Fanon Wiki, the free encyclopedia of jumpscares

So, Online Audacity Analog! is one of those annoying "software" screamers found on PlayScaryGames.fun. It tries to trick you into thinking it's a legit web version of Audacity, but it's just a setup for a jumpscare. It's actually pretty mean with how it baits you into clicking stuff.

How it works (The Bait)

When you load the page, it looks exactly like the old Audacity UI. You've got buttons for everything—Play, Record, Effects, the whole deal. But if you try to use it like normal software, it starts breaking down immediately.

The game uses a "strike" system to get you:

  • Click #1: If you click anything except the "Open" button, a basic browser alert pops up saying: "oops! something bad happened...."
  • Click #2: If you're stubborn and click again, the whole UI basically dies. Nothing works anymore except for the Reverse, Speed Change, and Distortion buttons (but they don't actually do anything to the audio).
  • Click #3: This is the trigger. As soon as you click a third time, the screamer hits.

The Payoff

On that third click, the UI disappears and you get a face-full of a creepy dude covered in black liquid. He's got a really wide mouth and he's staring right at you.

The sound is just the standard high-pitched scream from the original Scary Maze Game. Once the scream ends, the screen goes black and shows this text in big, white letters:

"NEVER TRUST FAKE AUDACITY ANALOGS, BUDDY!!!!!"

Trivia

  • A lot of people fall for this because they're looking for a quick way to edit audio without downloading the real Audacity.
  • The "Black Liquid Man" image is definitely a manipulated photo, maybe a 3D render or just a lot of Photoshop filters on a face.
  • It's not actually an "analog horror" thing, it just uses the word in the title for clicks.
  • If you just click "Open" and nothing else, you can stay on the page forever without getting scared, but you can't actually edit any music.
