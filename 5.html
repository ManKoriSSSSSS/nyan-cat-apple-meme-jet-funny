The Maze: Part 5

The Maze: Part 5 is a fan-made Flash screamer game currently hosted on the horror portal PlayScaryGames.fun. While the title suggests a long-running series, the game is actually a rescript and visual reskin of the original The Scary Maze Game by Jeremy Winterrowd.

Description

The gameplay mechanics are identical to the original maze game, requiring the player to navigate a cursor through a narrow path without touching the walls. However, the visual assets have been significantly altered:

  • Wall Color: The classic cyan walls have been replaced with a deep Vermillion.
  • The Goal: The end-point of each level is colored a vibrant Blue instead of the standard red.
  • Levels: The game features 4 playable levels, with the difficulty spike occurring during the 3rd and 4th stages.

The Screamer

Unlike the original game which uses the Exorcist girl (Regan MacNeil), The Maze: Part 5 features a different horror icon. As soon as the user completes the 4th level by reaching the blue goal, the screen is overtaken by an image of Glimpse (also known as the monster from SCP-087-B).

The visual jump-scare is accompanied by a distorted, high-decibel scream that persists until the browser window is closed or refreshed. Due to the high-frequency nature of the audio, it is categorized under high-intensity screamers in the Fanon archive.

Trivia

  • The game's URL on PlayScaryGames.fun often leads players to believe it is an official sequel to a non-existent Part 4.
  • This is one of the few maze reskins to use SCP-related imagery for its payoff.
  • The vermillion color palette was chosen specifically to cause more eye strain, making the 4th level harder to navigate.
